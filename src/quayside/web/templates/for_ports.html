How to join
  1. 01

    We set you up — no work on your end

    Quayside already collects publicly available auction data from ports across the UK. When you join, we pre-populate your dashboard with the data we already hold — so you can see your market position from day one.

    We've built the extraction pipeline, the normalisation layer, and the cross-port comparison engine. All you do is confirm the data is accurate. There is no setup, no integration, and no IT involvement required.

    No signup, no software, no cost.
  2. 02

    Review your data in a shared sheet

    We send you an online spreadsheet — an Excel or Google Sheet you can open from any browser, no software to install. Your daily price data is pre-filled by Quayside from public sources.

    Review the data, edit any cells that need correcting, then click the "Confirmed" button to let Quayside know it's ready to publish.

    Simple as editing a spreadsheet. That's all it takes.
  3. 03

    Your data goes live to subscribers

    Once confirmed, your prices are published to Quayside subscribers — fish buyers, merchants, and processors across the UK who rely on the daily digest for market intelligence.

    Subscribers receive daily price benchmarks, market trends, and species-level breakdowns. Your port's data contributes to the most complete picture of the UK fish market available anywhere.

Common questions
What format do you need?
We provide you with a shared online spreadsheet (Excel or Google Sheet) pre-filled with your data. Just review, correct anything that needs updating, and click "Confirmed". No special software or file formats required — it works from any browser.
What do other ports see?
Aggregated market ranges only — minimum, average, and maximum across all ports. No one sees your raw data, grades, or individual prices. When only two ports report a species, neither port is named.
What does it cost?
Nothing. Completely free. Quayside makes money from subscribers (buyers, sellers, processors) who pay for the aggregated data. Ports never pay.
Who else is on the platform?
Quayside launches in April 2026 with the goal of bringing the 20 most commercially active ports in the UK and Ireland onto the platform. More ports means better price transparency, fairer markets, and stronger benchmarks for everyone in the supply chain. Specific port partnerships will be announced closer to launch.
What if the extraction gets something wrong?
Every upload gets a confirmation email. One click to approve, or click to edit. We log corrections and improve our extraction automatically.
