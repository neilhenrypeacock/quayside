The problem

The UK fish market runs on incomplete information.

Every morning, fish merchants across the UK make buying decisions worth thousands of pounds — often without knowing what the same species sold for at a port 200 miles away an hour earlier.

The data exists. It's just scattered across five different websites, in four different formats, updated at different times.

  • 01 Five websites, every morning. Peterhead, Lerwick, Brixham, Newlyn, Scrabster — each with different formats, different update times, different species names.
  • 02 No cross-port comparison. You know what haddock costs at Peterhead. You don't know that Lerwick is 15% cheaper today unless you check both — which most merchants don't have time to do.
  • 03 No history, no trends. Is this price high or low for this time of year? For this species? There's no way to know without building your own spreadsheet over months.
  • 04 No equivalent product exists. This is not a solved problem. Quayside is the first UK-wide fish price aggregator.

What's haddock doing at Peterhead vs Lerwick over the last two weeks?
Peterhead has been consistently stronger. Over the past 14 days, A1 haddock averaged £2.79/kg at Peterhead vs £2.64/kg at Lerwick — a spread of around 6%. The gap widened this week, with Peterhead up 8% while Lerwick softened slightly. Volume at Peterhead is also higher, which is keeping the price firm.
Any species looking cheap right now relative to the last 30 days?
Coley and whiting are both below their 30-day averages. Coley is down around 11% across Scottish ports — likely oversupply from larger pelagic vessels. Whiting at Scrabster is at a 3-week low. If you're flexible on species, both represent reasonable value this week.
